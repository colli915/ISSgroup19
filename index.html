Socioeconomic Class and its Effect on Human Rights Enforcement

We chose this argument because in the past few years extending into the news today, human rights being neglected has become a common issue. There are many instances in the United States and around the world where all types of people are being treated wrongfully. Examples include but are not limited to people of color experiencing police brutality, gun violence in schools, and large scale poverty zones. We want to explore other areas where human rights are violated and the clearly apparent relationship these areas have with socioeconomic standing.

About

In the past, advocating for basic human rights was a large, impactful movement. Today, things have changed and society's rights are being picked away one by one. The people of America have grown up hearing about Rosa Parks refusing to give her seat on the bus because she was black. What about Brown v. Board of Education making segregation in schools unconstitutional? Many of the elder people of the United States have had first hand experience with President Theodore Roosevelt being one of the first government officials to advocate for universal health care. Where is the fight for rights that used to be so prominent? Some of the most common topics covered by modern media are gun violence, suppression of human rights in high poverty communities, the educational system, and most recently the impact that COVID-19 has on our lives. We decided to focus on the following topics because they all trace back to basic human rights. Gun violence is a leading cause of the suppression of human rights in urban communities and COVID-19 has caused job availability to decrease, leading to an increase in jobless citizens. There have been many research studies identifying unique patterns in social media data that may suggest the timings, locations, methods, and reasons behind occurrences of gun violence. This goes to show how an increase in media coverage can diminish our human rights. So why is mass media coverage still controlling our push for change? It is time for action, time to spread awareness, and time for change. Areas of low socioeconomic status should no longer be ignored, but be pushed to the forefront of the human rights movement.

Education in Prisons

This video shows how education in prisons can provide an incredibly impactful experience to inmates that choose to participate. Although they have a lower socioeconomic status, there is an opportunity provided to the prisoners that can be viewed as a second chance. The interviewed inmates describe how grateful they are to have access to their education rights, given their current circumstances. A feeling of hope is sparked within the students and they are able to realize that there are still possibilities of success after their time is served.
